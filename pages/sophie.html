Sophie

(October 2016 - March 2026)

In memory of my beloved dog Sophie, the best pup in the whole world.

All the Sophie Things 𓃢 𓃦 𓃥 𓁢

She got to play in the snow. She got to go to the beach house in York. She got to play on the beach and roll around in the sand. She got to take walks at the Boy Scout reservation. She got to stare at my duck collection. She got to go camping with us in White Mountains. She got to eat ALL her favorite treats. She got to eat bones and make a mess eating them on her bed. She got to chase you around the house, always catching up no matter how far ahead you were. She would trample down the stairs chasing a thrown toy. She would slip and slide on how wood floor when trying to run too fast inside. She would bark alongside Bruno and Gus whenever they heard something or saw something outside. She would jump a mile high into the air to catch a ball or toy. She got to bring special treats to her bed to eat them there. She got to learn and do all sorts of tricks: (Usually in this order for treats...) - Sit - Paw (and other paw... and other paw...) - Speak ("to the Heavens" or... "what do we say?") - Lie Down - Go to Sleep - Sit Up - Roll Over - Spin Around (while standing) - Heel - Stay (and wait a long time...) ("Okay Sophie you can have your treat now.") (And then later on...) - Close the Door - High Five - Play Keep Away - Ring the Bell (when she wanted to go outside) - Open the Door She got play fetch running down the stairs. She got to play hide and seek, sniffing around the living room until she found it. She got to get her zoomies out by running upstairs and running down and jumping all over the couch. She got to fluff the cushions on the couch to lay down, and anything else she sat on. She got to lay on her favorite bed in the corner of the living room. She got to play with all her toys including her favorite Lamby. She got to lay on my parent's bed every night to sleep. She got to lay on my bed sometimes, when she felt like it. She got to take long car rides and stick her head out the window. She got to see her cousins at parties and play with them. She got to jump in the pool as much as she wanted and roll around in the grass around to dry off. She got to be with my Mom all the time. She got to take walks. She got lots of love from everyone. She got to go to her favorite place my grandparent's house for vacation. She got to hide under my parent's bed whenever there was a storm, fireworks, wind, or anything that frightened her. She would hide under my bed too even though she could barely fit with everything underneath. She was afraid of so many silly thing: - Lights turning off. - People singing happy birthday. - Fan turning on in downstairs bathroom. She got to play with her shadow. Maybe too much. She got to play with leaves falling in autumn. She got to take a lap of our house, running through the woods, everytime she went outside. She got to chase bunnies and chipmunks and squirrels. She got to see so many animals: deers, fox, cats. She did not care for birds though. She got to run in the ocean, playing fetch. She got to catch balls in the air. She got to hop up on my parent's bed with all four legs at once. She got to snarl at me if I tried to pick her up by her belly. She got to scratch her back on the carpet by squirming on it She got to pop balloons and shred them to pieces. She got to lay in dirty laundry piles in the basement. She got to hide with my Dad by his desk, whenever someone was bothering her. She got to cause trouble, and then struggle to hide it with a guility look on her face. She got to eat an entire chicken, and then throw it up all over the floor. She got to be our favorite puppy forever. She got to give lots of kisses, maybe too many kisses. She got to give lots of hugs. She got to lay down on the couch with my Mom in the mornings. She got countless belly rubs. She got to greet everyone at the door with her tail wagging and butt wiggling. She got to jump off the rock wall next to our house whenever she did a lap of the house. She got to bark at strangers visiting our house. She got to scare the Amazon driver while he was delivering a package. She got to ignore other dogs or snarl at them (Sophie was not a fan of other dogs besides her cousins Bruno and Gus). She got to play tug of war, grabbing the toy so hard with her teeth that she could be pulled around in the air... still not letting go. She got to meet her brothers and sisters once, but was not a fan of them either. She got to jump up to give you kisses, even if you did not want it. She got to act like a human, sitting at the table, sitting on at the counter. She got to eat so much delicious food, and then beg for more. She got to open presents on Christmas day and her birthday, unwrapping gifts with her mouth. She got to be spoiled by everyone. She got to hold toys with her paw to keep you from taking them away. She got to be a part of our family and our lives for 9 years. She got to run around the lawn as much as she wanted. She got to talk when she was excited to see someone coming. She got to meet Kirthi and go to her old apartment in Boston. She got to be the best dog in the whole world. She gets to be remembered by everyone who knew and loved her. She gets to be known by everyone who hears her stories. She gets to be our little angel forever.
